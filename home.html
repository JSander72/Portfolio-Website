Self

As a self-taught software engineer, I have learned a lot through trial and error, and I wanted to share some of my insights with others who may be considering a similar path. Here are a few things I have learned along the way:

Stay up-to-date with the latest technologies and best practices. The field of software engineering is constantly evolving, and it is important to keep up with the latest trends and technologies. This may require dedicating some time each week to learning and staying current.

Don't be afraid to ask for help. While self-teaching can be a rewarding experience, it is important to remember that you don't have to go it alone. Don't be afraid to reach out to others in the industry or to seek help from online communities and forums.

Work

Practice, practice, practice. The best way to learn and improve your skills is to actually use them. Find projects to work on, whether they are personal projects or contributions to open source projects.

Network and make connections. Building relationships with others in the industry can be valuable for finding job opportunities, learning about new technologies, and getting feedback on your work.

Overall, being a self-taught software engineer requires dedication and a willingness to continuously learn and improve. It can be a challenging journey, but it can also be incredibly rewarding.
